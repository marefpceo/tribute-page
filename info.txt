Sir Timothy Jon Berners-Lee

Founder of the World Wide Web

1955 - born in London, England
1975 - Received a first-class Bachelor of Arts degree in physics from The Queen's College, Oxford
1978 - Created typesetting software for printers with D. G. Nash
1980 - Worked at The European Organization for Nuclear Research, CERN, as an independent contractor
1981 - Went to work at John Poole's Image Computer Systems, Ltd. to run the company's technical side
1984 - Returned to working at CERN
1989 - Wrote his proposal to join hypertext with the Internet
1990 - Published the first web site. A site explaining what the World Wide Web was and how to use it
1991 - Posted a public invitation on Usenet for collaboration of the World Wide Web project
1994 - Founded the W3C at the Massachusetts Institue of Technology
2001 - Became a patron of the East Dorset Heritage Trust
2004 - Accepted a chair in computer science at the University of Southampton
2007 - Elected as a member into the National Academy of Engineering
2013 - Awarded the inaugural Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering


I just had to take the hypertext idea and connect it to the TCP and DNS ideas and—ta-da!—the World Wide Web.

— Tim Berners-Lee[33]
